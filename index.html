Video world models · representation space

A video prior
does not belong
to its latent space.

We take a web-scale video diffusion model, lift it out of the pixel-VAE latent space it was trained in, and re-base it into a frozen DINOv3 representation space — where one model both generates video and predicts the semantic future without drifting.

Single mechanism: latent re-basing DINOv3-L · frozen Wan2.2-TI2V-5B · 30 blocks kept OpenVid-1M

Where this starts

DINOv3 sees a frame perfectly. It has never seen time.

A per-frame image encoder hands us clean, linearly-probeable state for every frame — for free, frozen. The open question is what to put between the frames.

Concatenating frame features is not a video representation. A video representation is a state you can roll forward. That is the object we are trying to build.

The task

Build a latent state that is compact, rollable, and double-readable.

Formally: learn pθ(zc+1:T | z1:c) over a latent that a frozen per-frame encoder defines — such that one sample decodes to pixels and reads out as semantics.

Requirement 01

Compact enough to model

Raw frame features are enormous and redundant. A learned linear bottleneck folds every 2×2×2 patch of features into one latent vector — 256× smaller, and short enough for a transformer to attend over.

Requirement 02

Smooth enough to roll

Autoregression compounds error. In a frozen self-supervised space, nearby states map to nearby latents, so a slightly-wrong prediction is still a valid state — the error gets absorbed instead of amplified.

Requirement 03

Rich enough to read twice

The same latent must decode to video (a generator) and, through frozen readout heads, to segmentation and depth (a predictor). One state, two exits.

One paragraph

Given a video stream, a frozen DINOv3 encoder E produces per-frame features. A learned codec (B, G) compresses them into a compact latent z and decodes them back. On that latent we train a world model pθ(zc+1:T | z1:c) — a distribution over the future, learned with flow matching, made autoregressive with a causal mask and per-frame noise levels. A single rollout is then evaluated on three orthogonal axes: generation (decode to pixels → VBench-2.0), prediction (frozen readout → future mIoU / depth), and drift (long-horizon error growth). Everything hinges on one design choice: where the latent lives, and what initializes the model on top of it.

Why this is hard

Both existing routes are broken — including ours.

The field has a generator that lives in the wrong space, and a predictor that has no prior. We hit the wall ourselves before we understood why.

Route A — pixel-latent generators

Web-scale, but predicting in a space built for reconstruction

Wan, CogVideoX and friends predict inside a VAE latent optimized for pixel fidelity, not for dynamics or semantics. Long autoregressive rollouts degrade into warped texture, and nothing in the latent can be read out as a scene.

Route B — representation-space world models

Right space, no prior, narrow domain

Recent feature-space world models are robust but trained from scratch on one domain — driving, or a single game. They cannot reach the general visual knowledge that lives inside a web-scale video model.

Route C — the split

Generation and prediction are two different systems

Generators cannot predict robustly; feature predictors (DINO-world, DINO-Foresight) are deterministic regressors and cannot generate. No single model does both — so no single model can be checked on both.

Our own wall — measured, not argued

A 100M from-scratch representation WM collapses to the mean

We exhaustively swept the parameterization axis (predict the change vs. predict the state). VBench-2.0 was brutal: the change-form goes static (dynamic degree 0.000), the state-form goes incoherent (subject consistency 0.594). Scaling capacity improved latent metrics while destroying downstream control.

Conclusion that redirected the project: the missing ingredient is not the parameterization. It is the prior.

Insight — click any card for the inspiration behind it

Pretraining was applied to the encoder. Nobody applied it to the generator.

Frozen-encoder world models call themselves "pretrained." Only half of them is. The dynamics model on top is still born from noise.

+
Insight 01 · about the space

Robustness is a property of the space, not of the trainer

Drift resistance comes from the smoothness of frozen self-supervised features: an imperfect prediction still lands on a plausible state. That property holds for any model running in that space.

Therefore — a generator does not have to be trained from scratch to inherit robustness. It only has to be moved into the space. This is what makes transplantation thinkable at all.

Insight 02 · about the prior

A video prior is knowledge about change, not about channels

What a video diffusion model learned — objects persist, motion is smooth, scenes stay consistent — is knowledge about how the visual world evolves. There is no reason it should be welded to the channel semantics of one particular VAE.

Therefore — swap the input and output projections, keep the transformer blocks, and the knowledge should survive the move. This is the falsifiable core of the project.

Inspiration · representation autoencoders for image diffusion; frozen-encoder latents shown to be generatable
+
Insight 03 · about the two jobs

Generation and prediction disagree on compression, not on space

Our negative result showed the two abilities want different parameterizations. But they can share one space — and trade off along a single dial: how much we compress it.

Therefore — do not choose. Keep one mechanism and expose compression as a knob: tight latents for generation, high-resolution latents for readout. Slide 10 turns this into a testable prediction.

The thesis

A web-scale video diffusion prior can be re-based out of its native pixel-VAE latent space into a frozen-DINOv3 representation space — and it will carry its dynamics knowledge with it. If true, every frontier video model is a world model waiting to be relocated.

Position

One new mechanism. Everything else is deliberately borrowed.

This is not a system paper. It is a single falsifiable question with the minimum apparatus needed to answer it.

New evaluation setting

One rollout. Three exits. Nobody currently passes all three.

The claim "single model, dual capability" is only interesting if it is checkable. So we make the same rollout answer to two independent authoritative benchmark families — plus one honest failure axis.

What is new here

Existing protocols evaluate a generator or a predictor. We hold the model fixed and force the same autoregressive sample through both authoritative pipelines — making "broad capability" an objective measurement rather than a narrative.

Architecture — click a stage

The whole system, end to end.

Three frozen components, two trained ones, one transplant. Nothing else.

Stage

Frozen per-frame encoder

DINOv3 ViT-L/16 at 224px, never updated. Multi-layer features are aggregated per frame into a 14×14×1024 grid. Freezing it is what makes the space smooth — and what makes the latents comparable across every arm of every experiment.

Component 1 of 2 · trained

The codec: compress the features, do not distort them.

A single strided convolution — a linear map per space-time patch. No nonlinearity, by design: we want compression, not a second representation.

Why linear

A nonlinear bottleneck would re-encode

Any nonlinearity risks inventing a new representation and throwing away the smoothness we came here for. A linear map can only compress.

Why 2× in time

The world model should not pay for redundancy

Adjacent frames are nearly identical. Folding pairs of frames halves the sequence the transformer must attend over — the difference between a toy and a 5B rollout.

Measured

18.6M params · trained in 10 minutes on one GPU

The codec is cheap and frozen afterwards. Every world-model arm in every experiment shares the exact same codec, the same latent statistics, and the same data — so arms are directly comparable.

Component 2 of 2 · the transplant

The surgery: keep the body, replace the sockets.

Baseline · faithful compression

Latent 7×7×32 — the transformer's own patchifier is discarded

The latent is already spatially compact, so the pretrained input conv (which expects a 2× patchify) no longer fits and must be re-initialized. We keep the 30 blocks, and that is where the prior lives.

Pre-registered prediction · H-J4

If the prior is the source of the advantage, transferring more of it should widen the gap

Switch the toggle to see the iteration: shape the latent like Wan's own input (48 channels, 14×14 grid) so its pretrained patchifier and output projection survive the move too. Falsifier: if the Wan-vs-scratch gap does not grow, then "amount of prior transferred" is not the mechanism, and the claim must shrink to "only the block attention matters."

Making a video generator into a world model

Two edits turn full attention into a streamable state.

The prior arrives non-causal and single-noise-level. It leaves frame-causal, per-frame-noise-level, and able to roll forward one frame at a time.

Edit 01 · causal attention

Bidirectional inside a frame, causal across frames

Tokens are laid out frame-major, so each frame is a contiguous block and a single additive mask makes the model causal in time. Verified: perturbing the last frame changes earlier outputs by exactly zero.

Edit 02 · diffusion forcing

Every frame gets its own noise level

During training each frame independently samples how noisy it is. The model therefore learns to denoise a frame while conditioning on cleaner past frames — which is exactly the situation it faces at rollout time. Clean context, noisy future, one network.

Training recipe

Freeze codec → encode clips → standardize latents → flow-matching loss with per-frame noise · AdamW, lr 5e-5, warmup + cosine, bf16, grad-clip 1.0, DDP. The only variable between the two headline arms is the transformer initialization.

What can go wrong — click a risk for the contingency

Each risk has a written exit, decided before the run.

Pre-registered fallbacks. Nothing here gets renegotiated after seeing the numbers.

+
Risk · resolution

7×7 latents are too coarse for segmentation readout

Competing predictors read out at 28×28 or finer. Our compact latent may simply not carry enough spatial detail to compete on Cityscapes / VSPW.

Contingency — split the compression dial (Insight 03): keep tight latents for the generation axis, re-base a second arm into a low-compression 14×14 latent for the prediction axis. The prior-aligned codec on slide 10 already delivers this for free, and doubles as the H-J4 test.

+
Risk · ceiling

VBench scores the pixel decoder, not the world model

The generation axis is upper-bounded by how well the RAE decoder renders. A weak decoder can hide a strong world model.

Contingency — always evaluate a ground-truth-decoded control set: the same decoder rendering real latents. That measures the ceiling explicitly. If the ceiling is the binding constraint, the generation claim is downgraded to secondary and prediction (which needs no decoder) carries the paper.

+
Risk · mechanism

The advantage is the architecture, not the prior

Maybe Wan's block design simply happens to be good, and initialization is incidental.

Contingency — the scratch arm is architecturally identical, so this is already controlled. H-J4 pushes further: transferring more pretrained parameters must widen the gap. If it does not, we shrink the claim honestly to "the block design transfers, the weights do not."

+
Risk · infrastructure

The cluster is full; the flagship cannot start

Large runs are queued behind other users. Wall-clock, not GPU budget, is the binding constraint.

Contingency — the science was deliberately front-loaded onto cheap runs: the decisive gate cost two GPUs. Scale-up buys strength, not validity. Meanwhile the cheapest authoritative evaluations (physics, readout heads) run on one GPU and proceed regardless.

Reading

Not just faster — better

The gap does not close by convergence. The transplanted model reaches a strictly better endpoint, predicts more accurately under autoregression, and degrades more slowly the further it rolls. Scaling the run further (30k steps, larger batch) lowers the loss again to 0.255 — the advantage keeps cashing in.

Reported honestly — on IntPhys2 physical-plausibility, both arms sit at chance (AUROC ≈ 0.50). The prior transfers dynamics accuracy and drift robustness; it does not transfer physical reasoning. Physics is therefore not a benchmark we claim, and we say so before anyone asks.
